A Thousand Year Old Vampire

A Solo RPG Playthrough


Name: Zhou Mingzhe (周明哲)

Birth Year: 847 AD

Origin: Chang’an, China


Background:

Born the son of a minor tax collector, Zhou became a tutor of a merchant family after failing the Imperial exams multiple times, bringing shame to his family’s name. He nearly died of starvation in a devastating famine during the reign of Emperor Xizong in 874 AD. A humble coffin-maker found him and, accidentally thinking Zhou was dead, attempted to prepare him for burial. However, he was interrupted by a group of pillagers who sacked his workshop and murdered him. Due to the burial rites not being properly completed, Zhou rose from death as a Jiangshi, the hopping undead. His lower body rigid and stiff, he is cursed to hobble throughout the land at the mercy of strangers, forever tormented by a terrible hunger for their qi. This is his story...

Memories

Memory 1

  • Out of gratitude, a lowly fisherman offers to serve me after I "cured" his daughter of an agonizing plague.
  • After the fisherman expressed his gratitude, I feel disgusted by the horrid rotting leech I've become and ashamed by the way I've taken advantage of others; I isolate myself from the other monks and never leave the monastary, continuing to feed on the dying.
  • One of the monks catch me in the act of feeding and I visciously attack and injure him; I flee with Gao across the waters to the island of Taiwan and adopt the name of Jiànhóng Li (907 AD)

Memory 2

  • My best calligraphy to date was so beautiful, Master Chen almost passed out at the mere sight of it

Memory 3

  • After failing the exams, my father threw my examination papers at me in anger; the disgusted look on his face still breaks my heart

Memory 4

  • I recite the poem Quiet Night Thoughts to myself while residing in the capital city for the exams to help me sleep, my heart longing for home

Memory 5

  • Due to tightening travel restrictions in response to recent rebellions, I am almost found out by officials at a travel checkpoint; after unsuccessfully using Confucian reasoning to get through, one of the clerks agrees to let me pass, for a price of course...
  • I am found out by another Jiangshi by the name of Tou Yong who steals my donkey Hui, although I put up a fight and gave him a nasty scar before he ran off.
  • Stranded in the Fujian province, a local Buddhist temple takes me under their wing as a hospice worker; when a wealthy donor's relative starts recovering, I ensure they die to keep the donations flowing.

Diary

Memory 1

  • Hardly clinging to life, I hazily watch as the two thugs viscously beat the coffin-maker to death with clubs before I sink into eternal darkness
  • Having no one else to turn to, I drag myself through the streets until I reach Master Chen’s house; having compassion on me for my plight, he gives me one of his donkeys to help me get around.
  • To feed without detection, I pose as a traveling physician and “cure” the dying or terminally ill that I find; they’re on their way out anyway and the families are grateful.

Memory 2

  • I am Zhou Mingze, born in Chang’an to a tax collector during the reign of Emperor Xuanzong of Tang; I failed the imperial exam and brought eternal shame to my family.

Characters

  • Master Chen: my Confucian tutor, still alive in Chang'an
  • Zhou Wei: my younger brother, now head of the family
  • Li Cheng: the merchant's son and my appointed pupil, rather dull-witted
  • Chen Mu: the coffin-maker who attempted to bury me, also turned Jiangshi after his violent demise
  • Li Kang: a corrupt checkpoint official who lets me off after I agree to pay him a bribe
  • Tou Yong: a wandering Jiangshi living on the side of the road who made off with my donkey
  • Gao Zemin: a lowly fisherman who has offered to be my servant

Skills

  • Master Calligrapher: naturally talented and able to create beautiful scripts with brush and ink
  • Learned Litterateur: knows all classic Chinese literature both inside and out
  • Confucian Scholar: Very well versed in the teachings of Confucius, much to my chagrin as I know exactly what burial rites were botched on me.
  • Careful Coroner: Able to medically determine if someone is terminally ill or near death so I can target them for feeding
  • Dodging Beuracracy: Can manipulate people or find loopholes to get around certain laws
  • Deathly Fury: Will fight like a dragon to defend myself and those I hold dear

Resources

  • Worn Book of Tang Poetry
  • Examination Essays
  • Old calligraphy brush
  • An old donkey named Hui
  • Diary - A handscroll made of mulberry paper
  • Hospice Ledger - To keep track of all the Temple patients I treat, especially those with deep pockets.
  • Buddhist Temple - My new home
